## Answer Key

1. c) 80 or above
2. b) Second wave
3. b) Ethiopia
4. b) Peaberry
5. c) Natural
6. c) Roasters buying directly from producers, without a formal certification
7. b) Immersion
8. c) Flat
9. b) 7–10 days
10. b) A roasting stage after which beans become oily with heavy smoky flavor
11. False — expensive doesn't always mean better; terroir, sourcing, and marketing all play a role.
12. False — a 12 oz drip typically has more total caffeine than a double shot of espresso, since espresso is more concentrated, not higher in total caffeine.
13. True
14. True
15. False — organic status isn't a reliable flavor indicator; many excellent coffees aren't certified organic.
16. Under-extraction happens when not enough is pulled from the grounds (too little water/time), producing sour, face-puckering flavors. Over-extraction happens when too much is pulled (too much water/time), producing bitterness and a lingering finish. Good extraction balances the two.
17. **Washed** — fruit removed down to the parchment before drying; clean, classic flavor with minimal fermentation. **Honey** — mucilage left on during drying after skin/pulp removal; sweeter than washed, less wild than natural. **Natural** — cherry dried whole with fruit intact; heavy fermentation, fruity, wine-like flavors.
18. Order an espresso, a cappuccino, and a filter brew (pour-over or drip) at the same café. The espresso shows how well they dial in extraction; the cappuccino shows their milk texturing skill; the filter brew shows how the coffee tastes on its own, unmasked by milk or pressure.
19. Modern blends often combine two high-quality, traceable coffees (rather than commodity "kitchen sink" coffee) to create a new flavor profile — often more chocolatey and round — that can't be achieved from a single origin alone, while still crediting the producers on the label.
20. Dialing in is the ongoing process of tasting a brew and adjusting parameters (most often grind size) to reach a target result. The three tracked parameters are input (dose), output (yield), and time.
